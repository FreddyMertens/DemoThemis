User testing packet

Game-theory lab QA form

Use this document while testing the interactive demothemis game board. The goal is to catch broken controls, confusing explanations, inaccurate protocol logic, bad mental models, accessibility issues, and places where users leave with the wrong game-theory lesson.

Testing target: game-theory.html. Fill the form, then click Save filled HTML. The saved copy contains the answers inside the HTML itself, so it can be emailed, archived, reopened, printed, or reviewed without browser storage.

4. Scenario tests

Use the presets and then manually stress the dials. The tester should say what changed and why.

Balanced court baseline

  1. Click `Balanced court`.
  2. Ask what the top four score cards mean.
  3. Ask which attack is the current bottleneck.
  4. Move `Active jurors` up and down.

Cold start thin pool

  1. Click `Cold start`.
  2. Ask why the court is more fragile.
  3. Increase active jurors and observe the board.
  4. Check whether the copy explains why low-friction entry matters.

Demand surge workload shock

  1. Click `Demand surge`.
  2. Move settlement flow higher.
  3. Move active jurors lower and higher.
  4. Ask why pay pressure and live reserve move.

Bloc attack capture pressure

  1. Click `Bloc attack`.
  2. Increase coordinated humans.
  3. Increase active jurors.
  4. Increase typical contested stake until panel size changes.
  5. Ask what actually stops the bloc.

Sleepy market watcher economics

  1. Click `Sleepy market`.
  2. Raise cost to watch one market.
  3. Toggle `Paid watchers` off and on.
  4. Ask why false assertions become dangerous.

Break the locks failure mode

  1. Click `Break the locks`.
  2. Turn locks on one by one.
  3. Ask which lock gives the biggest safety jump.
  4. Ask whether any lock feels redundant or unexplained.
