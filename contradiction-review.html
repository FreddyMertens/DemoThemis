C-02

The deployed 3/3 court cannot self-recover from a quorum miss and stalls after a no-show

Critical
Promised behavior

Cases “must never brick”; a missed quorum gets one redraw and then status quo. The official queue is presented as a repeatable end-to-end court.

Legacy deployed behavior

Non-revealers are fully slashed, deactivated, and excluded from the redraw. With only three active jurors, a quorum miss leaves at most one eligible juror, so the promised redraw cannot fill. One no-show also leaves only two active for the next case.

Why it is probably there

Redraw tests use the 7/14 cohort buffer, while mainnet deploys pool = panel. The UI and keeper require exactly three, so test assumptions do not match deployment assumptions.

Best handling

Implemented in the replacement source: preserve the full slash, let the verified human post a fresh Permit2 bond, allow them back into the retry, and finalize status quo permissionlessly if the fixed recovery deadline expires. Accept pools wider than three, deploy the three-seat replacement with at least four eligible jurors for a one-withdrawal availability reserve, and retain exact-3/3 lifecycle tests. A fresh deployment is still required.

Remediation status, 22 July 2026: implemented locally in court liveness version 2, registry version 1, the keeper, Mini App, and the 100-test suite with exact-3/3 regressions. The immutable Step-5 addresses remain affected and are now labeled legacy; the capstone is paused pending a recommended three-seat / minimum-four redeployment and cutover. See DemoThemisMVP/docs/LIVENESS_RECOVERY.md.

Original evidence: DisputeCourt.sol:311-349; JurorRegistry.sol:157-169,190-198; mainnet-question-keeper.mjs:362-369,463-488; MECHANISM_DELTA.md:31,36; contracts/test/DisputeCourt.t.sol:139-191.

C-03

Party exclusion can make a case permanently undrawable and strand escrow principal

Critical
Promised behavior

Both case types ship; escrow settlement is atomic; re-arming prevents cases from bricking.

Legacy deployed behavior

Opening checks raw active count, but drawing excludes the opener/payer/payee. A case can therefore be accepted with fewer than three eligible people. Once a deal is Disputed, only the court can settle it; there is no timeout, cancel, or refund path.

Why it is probably there

MIN_POOL was used as a proxy for eligibility, and “re-arm” was treated as recovery even when the eligible set could never become large enough.

Best handling

Implemented in the replacement source: compute the active eligible count after excluding distinct parties and reject before question fees or escrow state lock; give every accepted case an immutable one-hour first-draw deadline; and let anyone unwind after expiry. The whole unused fee returns to the opener/payer, while an escrow timeout also returns principal to its payer. InitialDrawTimedOut marks cancellation with no merits ruling—never question NO. Use MIN_POOL >= PANEL_SIZE + 1 (4 for 3 seats) for one pre-draw withdrawal of adjudication capacity; exact 3 remains fund-safe through bounded unwind. A fresh deployment is still required.

Remediation status, 22 July 2026: implemented locally in court liveness version 2, the keeper, Mini App, and exact-3/3 regressions. Registry liveness remains version 1. Opening failure is atomic for both case types; failed draws cannot extend the first-draw deadline; the unwind is permissionless and idempotent. Its direct refund assumes immutable trusted MockUSD cannot pause or blacklist the payer; generalized production tokens should use pull/claim credits. The authenticated keeper ignores nonofficial cases for queue sequencing, so refundable outsider spam cannot censor the official queue. The immutable Step-5 addresses remain affected and must not receive capstone funds or jurors.

Original evidence: DisputeCourt.sol:175-198,241-265; DealEscrow.sol:109-136; contracts/test/CourtEdges.t.sol:75-113; INDEPENDENT_REVIEW.md:32-37,60-62. Replacement evidence: DisputeCourt.sol:30-40,114-124,202-270,352-371,509-516; contracts/test/InitialDrawLiveness.t.sol; docs/LIVENESS_RECOVERY.md.

H-07

A ten-cent market was promised a human ruling it could not fund

High
Previous promise

Start with $0.10; every market gets a human ruling; “nothing is subsidized.”

Economic limit

The jury has a natural work price. Even a 100% deduction from $0.10 cannot pay a $20 ruling, so opening a market and funding its resolution cannot be the same promise.

Why it was probably there

Permissionless creation and independent court pricing were designed as separate value propositions, but the funding gate between them was omitted.

Implemented handling

If it is short, the creator may top up; otherwise no jury is drawn, no market fee is charged, and every pooled YES/NO position receives its backing at its recorded purchase price.

Remediation status, 22 July 2026: implemented in the market chapter, interactive reserve example, robustness/tokenization gate, full run-through state diagram, bootstrap/economics copy, and prediction-market design note. The dime now creates a market, not an unfunded promise of human work.
