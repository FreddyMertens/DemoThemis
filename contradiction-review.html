Contradictions a reviewer can encounter

Evidence names the public page or visible interaction rather than internal project files.

H-01

“Unbuyable” and “bullet proof” are absolute, but the public attack model shows non-zero capture risk

High
Public promise

Headings and product copy repeatedly call the court unbuyable, bullet proof, ungameable, or something whales cannot buy.

Public model

Break the Court calculates probabilities and conditions under which capture remains possible, especially with a narrow pool or disabled defenses.

Why

Memorable positioning hardened into a literal security guarantee while the model correctly remained probabilistic.

Handle

Use “designed to make capture impractical under stated assumptions,” then link directly to the modeled conditions and residual risk.

Visible evidence: Home product cards; DemoThemis “What makes the court unbuyable”; OmenMarketMaker arbitration headings; Break the Court results.

M-01

The products are called independent, optional, and mutually required

Medium
Independence

DemoThemis says OmenMarketMaker is one launch path, not a permanent dependency, and both products keep separate contracts, governance, and treasuries.

Mutual requirement

The Home page says both products require the other to exist, while other public copy presents the court as reusable by any app.

Why

A temporary bootstrap relationship is compressed into an unconditional product dependency.

Handle

Say OmenMarketMaker is the initial demand source and reference integration, while DemoThemis is intended to become independently reusable.

Visible evidence: Home product introduction; DemoThemis Bootstrap section; Bootstrap Loop; OmenMarketMaker resolution boundary.

M-02

Market graduation uses incompatible thresholds and boundary operators

Medium
Robustness Lock

It displays more than $5,000 per side, more than $1,000 of standing offers, and a 24-hour hold. Its interaction actually passes at exactly those dollar values.

Growth demo

The earlier interactive market graduates around $50 per side, accepts any live offer, and does not require the same visible 24-hour step.

Why

The teaching demo and the claimed canonical lock use different scales and logic without labeling one as compressed.

Handle

Publish one canonical rule and drive both widgets from it. If the first widget is illustrative, label its values and preserve the same logical checks.

Visible evidence: OmenMarketMaker “Liquidity Flywheel” interaction and “The Robustness Lock” interaction.

M-03

“One human, one vote” conflicts with reputation-dependent vote weight

Medium
Equality claim

DemoThemis says it replaces token voting with one human, one vote and that money never buys more voice for one person.

Reputation rule

The public reputation table says “full vote weight” is earned only when a juror’s private interval clears 0.70.

Why

“One human, one vote” is being used for both identity uniqueness and equal tally weight, although the design changes influence by reputation.

Handle

Say “one human, one seat” if tally weights differ, or keep every seated ballot equal and let reputation affect only eligibility, draw rate, and pay.

Visible evidence: DemoThemis “The wrong foundation” and “The reputation dial.”

M-04

The early-bettor bonus is promised but not carried through tokenization and payout

Medium
Bonus promise

OmenMarketMaker and the run-through say an early $50 position counts like $54 when winnings are divided.

Visible mechanics

The pool widget divides the gross pool only by side totals, ordinary fungible YES/NO tokens do not retain purchase time, and the final receipt shows no early-entry allocation.

Why

A pool-era time weight was retained after positions became freely fungible tokens.

Handle

Either remove the bonus or represent it as a separate non-transferable claim with an explicit, conserved settlement formula.

Visible evidence: OmenMarketMaker “Start a market” and tokenization sections; Run-through first trade, token conversion, and final payout.

M-05

The current 2% market fee exceeds Governance’s stated 0–1% range

Medium
Omen economics

The economics table and run-through receipt charge a 2% OmenMarketMaker settlement fee.

Governance rail

The Governance chapter says the market token may govern its own fee and margin only inside 0–1%.

Why

The operating example and constitutional range were updated independently.

Handle

Choose one number. Either lower the example to at most 1% or amend the public governance range and explain the higher ceiling.

Visible evidence: OmenMarketMaker economics table; Run-through final payout; Governance “What a captured vote still could not do.”

M-06

The private-room winner is promised the gross pool before mandatory deductions

Medium
Room terms

Two $10,000 stakes visibly promise “Winner receives $20,000.”

Shared settlement rule

The same public design says YES/NO must reimburse the resolution bond pro-rata and OmenMarketMaker charges a settlement fee.

Why

The private-room ticket still displays gross escrow as the winner’s net receipt.

Handle

Show gross pool, bond reimbursement, market fee, and estimated net payout as separate lines before both parties sign.

Visible evidence: OmenMarketMaker “Private markets”; Run-through private-room creation and funding screens; public resolution economics.

M-07

A top-tier panel split is appealable even though the public ladder has no larger rung

Medium
Parallel-panel rule

Several 31-seat panels handle the largest cases; disagreement escalates or becomes provisional insufficient information that may be appealed.

Appeal ceiling

The same chapter says the ordinary ladder ends at 31 seats and the 31-seat rung becomes final.

Why

The single-panel appeal ladder was applied to a separate multi-panel ceiling without defining a next step.

Handle

Define a larger top-tier rule—such as more disjoint 31-seat panels—or make top-tier disagreement terminal and specify its market settlement.

Visible evidence: DemoThemis ceiling simulator and appeal ladder; Run-through “Provisional INSUFFICIENT_INFORMATION” branch.

L-01

“Tradeable through resolution” omits the smaller-pool exception

Low
Broad claim

Home and OmenMarketMaker advertise that positions remain tradeable throughout resolution.

Actual public design

Only markets that graduate into wallet-held tokens have that property; smaller markets remain internal pool claims.

Why

A benefit of graduated markets is written as a universal market feature.

Handle

Add “for tokenized markets” everywhere the through-resolution trading benefit is promised.

Visible evidence: Home feature list; OmenMarketMaker feature summary, tokenization, and “Markets keep trading while resolution runs.”

L-02

The resolved-case testing floor is 5%, but Break the Court defaults to 4% and allows 0%

Low
Governance constitution

Resolved-case tests must remain between 5% and 20% of the score window.

Public model control

Break the Court’s full model exposes “Real cases used in aptitude tests” from 0% to 25%, defaulting to 4%.

Why

The model’s editable range predates the constitutional bound.

Handle

Set the visible model range to 5–20% and choose a default inside it, or label the control as an intentionally unconstitutional stress test.

Visible evidence: Governance parameter table; Break the Court “Edit full model” → “Real cases used in aptitude tests.”

L-03

The public model requires “enough stake” while the chapter rejects stake-based entry

Low
Model wording

Break the Court describes drawable jurors as active, online, holding enough stake, and eligible.

Product explanation

DemoThemis says personhood creates the seat and money must not buy court power; its economic discipline is private money at risk tied to work.

Why

“Stake” survived as shorthand for the private work-risk requirement and now sounds like token-gated eligibility.

Handle

Replace it with “meeting the private money-at-risk requirement” and keep that requirement distinct from personhood and voting power.

Visible evidence: Break the Court full-model help for “Active jurors available for drawing”; DemoThemis identity and reserve explanations.

L-04

The run-through says evidence rules are locked but never shows them

Low
Lifecycle claim

The run-through repeatedly says the question and evidence rules lock before betting and move into the court case.

Playable market

The creation form shows a resolve condition but no admissible sources, cutoff, evidence standard, or visible evidence-policy field.

Why

The simulator models the rule as background state instead of a reviewer-visible market term.

Handle

Add one concise “Evidence policy” field to creation, resolution request, and court intake so the same rule can be followed across the handoff.

Visible evidence: Run-through lifecycle map; Create Market, Resolution Request, and Court Intake screens.

L-05

Two appeal prompts still describe the whole contribution as the returnable bond

Low
Correct breakdown

The appeal screen separates a non-refundable service-fee share from returnable or forfeitable security principal.

Prompts

The walkthrough summary and contribution coach say the wallet owns a percentage of whatever “that bond” or “returned bond or loss” later produces.

Why

Short instructional text retained the old single-bond vocabulary after the visible receipt was split correctly.

Handle

Say the wallet funds both components pro-rata but receives or forfeits only its security-principal share.

Visible evidence: Run-through Appeal Ladder overview, contribution coach, and Crowdfunded Appeal Funding receipt.

L-06

“Orb verification is the only eligibility gate” is broader than the design shown below it

Low
Absolute sentence

DemoThemis says Orb verification is the only eligibility gate.

Other visible gates

A draw-bound presence check must pass before voting, reputation affects draw eligibility, and 31-seat panels require proof of long service.

Why

The sentence means “only identity-enrollment gate,” but it reads as “only condition for serving.”

Handle

Change it to “Orb verification is the only identity-enrollment gate,” then list presence, conflicts, availability, and panel-specific qualification separately.

Visible evidence: DemoThemis “The same person, every time,” privacy proof list, 31-seat eligibility, and reputation dial.
