MVP versus funded roadmap

What this demo proves—and what comes next

The core court is deployed today. The roadmap hardens privacy, randomness, panel security, and adoption without blurring what is already live.

Capability
Deployed MVP World Chain · source-verified
Funded roadmap → Milestone-gated upgrades
01Identity & draw
Live on-chain Verified personhood, limited draw

World ID 4.0, a nullifier sybil gate, wallet-bound proofs, and a two-step post-question draw for the three-seat demo.

M1 Verifiable production randomness

VRF or drand replaces the current blockhash-based seed, paired with correctness and verifier hardening.

02Ballot privacy
Live on-chain Sealed commit/reveal ballots

Votes stay hidden until reveal, then become public and receipt-ful so the full ruling can execute on-chain.

M2 Receipt-free private ballots

MACI-style encrypted voting hides juror identity and vote before and after the aggregate tally.

03Panel security
Live on-chain One three-seat panel

Uniform selection proves the end-to-end mechanism, without reputation weighting, appeals, or parallel panels.

M3b Security that scales with stakes

Private Wilson-gated reputation, 7→15→31 appeals, and independent parallel panels widen the security regime.

04Settlement & reach
Live on-chain Atomic settlement by rule

The majority closes the case and its locked fee settles with no admin override.

M3a–M6 Demand-priced, reusable court

Processing, panel work, reserve top-up, and capped operations form each quote; an optimistic fast path and resolution SDK broaden its reach.

Clear boundary

Roadmap capabilities are not presented as active in the live interface. They are the milestone-gated path from a working three-person court to production-scale resolution.
