The bootstrap loop

How PredictionMoMo bootstraps the court

PredictionMoMo creates paid resolution work from day one, then turns settled markets into evidence DemoThemis can use to win outside disputes.

Bootstrap loop 6 min read • Chapter 5 of 6

The trap

The cold start arbitration cannot dodge

A court needs cases to build a record and a record to attract cases. PredictionMoMo supplies paid work, juror revenue, and public proof before outside apps take the first risk.

The way in

The first paying customer is a bet

PredictionMoMo must resolve elections, matches, prices, and private bets before paying out. Those locked markets give DemoThemis cases, fees for jurors, and objective results that become resolved cases for aptitude tests.

The money

Fees pay the judges, not a printing press

A 2% settlement fee routes 70% to juror pay, 20% to the reward pool, and 10% to operations. Revenue rises with use rather than committing the court to a fixed payroll.

Resolved cases for aptitude tests

Settled markets keep the court tied to reality

The hybrid system uses leave-one-out agreement for everyday scoring, but a whole panel can share the same error. Objective results—a final score, timestamped price, or election—give the court an outside audit.

Only independently checkable outcomes belong in this stream. Subjective, ambiguous, manipulated, or poorly specified markets require a rubric court or exclusion; settlement alone does not turn them into ground truth.

Illustrative: a rational watcher covers a window only when the expected bounty exceeds the cost. Thin markets can still go unwatched when that condition fails.

The model is an incentive test, not a coverage guarantee. Real deployment must measure watcher concentration, alert latency, capital requirements, and whether several watchers independently cover each window.

A watched settlement still needs enough volume, a clean window, real coverage, and a meaning check before becoming a test. Public cases audit the pool; consented private-room cases can audit individual jurors only when those jurors never participated and cannot look up the answer.

The climb

Three phases, each standing on the last

PredictionMoMo is the first rung; its record lets DemoThemis move into wider disputes.

  1. 1

    Markets only

    Market fees pay jurors, settled outcomes grade them, and fair calls build a public aggregate court record.

  2. 2

    Open the doors to real disputes

    The protocol adds escrowed work, creator payouts, marketplace refunds, and app-specific disputes.

  3. 3

    Become the default

    Signed verdict receipts and measured accuracy let new apps verify the court instead of rebuilding support desks.

Why it compounds

One wheel, not three repeated stories

Each turn supplies both demand for the court and evidence that can win the next customer.
