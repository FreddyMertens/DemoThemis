How to play

There are no magic constants here

The game is simple: every number either comes from traffic, juror supply, a fee split, or an attacker. If a number cannot explain what it tracks, it does not belong on the board.

The lab is not a parameter vote and it is not a promise about launch volumes. It is a map of dependencies. Demand creates fee flow. Fee flow creates juror pay pressure. Pay pressure and unresolved workload create the live reserve. The reserve deters laziness but, if made too heavy, thins the juror pool. Pool width, panel size, sealed randomness, receipt-free ballots, face checks, answer keys, watched assertions, appeal floors, and governance all press on the final question: does a bad strategy make money?

Every variable

What sets what

Settlement flow

External demand. It feeds fee revenue, watcher bounties, answer keys, and the work available to jurors.

Challenge rate

The share of markets that need jurors. Higher challenge rate raises court workload and lowers the number of quiet answer keys.

Active jurors

The supply side. More active jurors spread work, lower per-juror pay pressure, widen the pool, and reduce bloc capture odds.

Panel size

Stake chooses seats. Bigger cases get larger panels, so an attacker must win more draws at once.

Fee split

A routing rule, not a fixed payout. It decides how fee flow becomes pay, rewards, and operations.

Pay pressure

Fee flow divided by casework and active supply. It tells the system how attractive juror work is right now.

Live reserve

Pending income at risk divided by careless-error slash pressure. It rises with workload and falls when supply catches up.

Reward pool

Funded by the fee split and slashes. It pays quality over time instead of paying any one verdict to punish dissent.

Sealed draw

Turns panel selection from grindable retries into one public, verifiable roll bound to the case.

Receipt-free ballot

Destroys the bribe market. If a briber cannot verify delivery, the bribe contract cannot clear.

Face check

Makes a rented credential useless at the exact moment the vote unlocks.

Answer keys

Settled public outcomes and consented private tests give the court an external signal against drift.

Watchers

A false assertion creates a bounty. Coverage rises when the reward beats the cost of staying awake.

Appeal floor

Each rung must pay the next panel, price capture odds, and rent the delay it imposes.

Petition gate

Loser-side signatures price sincerity without letting winners block the only people motivated to appeal.

Governance

Token voice is checked by verified participation and product use, so a pure wallet cannot simply buy the rules.

The equations

The shortest version of the math

Economic flow

fee revenue = settlement flow × settlement fee
juror pay pool = fee revenue × pay share
pay pressure = juror pay pool / active jurors

Reserve sizing

reserve unit = pay per vote / (extra wrong-rate × slash fraction)
live reserve = reserve unit × unresolved votes per juror

Capture math

bloc share = coordinated humans / active jurors
capture odds = chance a random panel draws a majority from that share
sealed draw makes attempts = 1

Verdict efficacy

efficacy rises with juror skill, answer keys, watchers, and governance health
efficacy falls with capture odds, enforceable bribes, cheap rentals, lazy profit, and cheap re-rolls

Pressure ranking

The board tells you what is load-bearing

When the model gets stressed, the highest bar below is the thing to fix first. If it is capture, widen the pool or increase panel hardness. If it is drift, you need more keys. If it is laziness, reserve sizing is too weak. If it is assertions, watcher economics are not paying for the night shift.

The takeaway

The safe design is the one where bad strategies do not clear

A high reserve is not automatically safer. A high fee is not automatically better. A bigger panel is not free. A stricter gate can make jurors herd. Every value has a job and a cost. The design works when each variable tracks the thing it is meant to track and refuses every other job.

The protocol's central loop is this: traffic pays for jurors, jurors settle contested traffic, settled traffic grades jurors, and the grading makes the next verdict harder to buy. The game board above is just that loop with the attackers left on the table.
