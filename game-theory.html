How to play

Most outputs trace back to visible inputs and open assumptions

The lab is simple on the surface: most outputs trace back to traffic, juror supply, a fee split, or an attacker. The advanced drawer opens the modeling assumptions behind the launch-stage index so the fixed factors are visible instead of implied.

The lab is not a parameter vote, a promise about launch volumes, or a measured failure probability. It maps the launch-stage incentive loop around protocol-held escrow. Demand creates fee flow. Fee flow creates juror pay pressure. Pay pressure and unresolved workload create the live reserve. The reserve deters laziness but, if made too heavy, thins the juror pool. Pool width, panel size, sealed randomness, receipt-free ballots, face checks, answer keys, watched assertions, and appeal floors all press on the final question: does a bad strategy make money?

Visible variables

What the current board exposes

Settlement flow

External demand routed through protocol-held escrow. It feeds fee revenue, watcher bounties, competency tests, and the work available to jurors.

Challenge rate

The share of the settlement flow that escalates to court. The rest are "quiet cases" that settle automatically without jurors, but still pay the system fee to subsidize the court.

Active jurors

The supply side before reserve burden. Heavy reserves can price some out; rewards and pay can retain them.

Panel size

Stake chooses seats. Bigger cases use larger, more senior pools and can require parallel panels.

Fee split

A routing rule, not a fixed payout. It decides how fee flow becomes pay, rewards, and operations.

Pay pressure

The total fees and rewards available per active juror. It measures how attractive the work is, naturally recruiting more people. It is not a guaranteed wage.

Live reserve

Pending income at risk divided by careless-error slash pressure. It rises with workload and falls when supply catches up.

Reward pool

Funded by the fee split and slashes. It pays quality over time instead of paying any one verdict to punish dissent.

Sealed draw

Turns panel selection from grindable retries into one public, verifiable roll bound to the case.

Receipt-free ballot

Weakens the bribe market. If a briber cannot verify delivery, the bribe contract is much harder to enforce.

Face check

Narrows a rented credential to a live-person check at the moment the vote unlocks.

Competency tests

Settled public outcomes and consented private tests give the court an external signal against drift.

Watchers

A false assertion creates a bounty. Coverage needs positive watcher EV and enough capacity for all settlement windows.

Appeal floor

Each rung must pay the next panel, price capture odds, and rent the delay it imposes.

Petition gate

Loser-side signatures price sincerity without letting winners block the only people motivated to appeal.

The equations

The shortest version of the math

Economic flow

fee revenue = settlement flow × settlement fee
juror pay pool = fee revenue × pay share
reward pool = fee revenue × reward share
retained jurors respond to pay, rewards, and reserve burden

Reserve sizing

reserve unit = pay per vote / (extra wrong-rate × slash fraction)
live reserve = reserve unit × unresolved votes per juror
if the burden gets too heavy, the effective pool thins

Capture math

bloc share = coordinated humans / eligible draw pool
capture estimate = finite draw chance of a panel majority
large stakes automatically require parallel panels

Illustrative index

index rises with juror skill, curated tests, paid watchers, and reward-funded retention
index falls with active risks, and any attack that clears caps the score

Pressure ranking

The board tells you what is stressed

When the model gets stressed, the highest bar below is the first pressure point to inspect. If it is capture, widen the pool or increase panel hardness. If it is drift, you need more competency tests. If it is laziness, reserve sizing is too weak. If it is assertions, watcher economics are not paying for the night shift.

The takeaway

The target is a loop where bad strategies do not clear

A high reserve is not automatically safer. A high fee is not automatically better. A bigger panel is not free. A stricter gate can make jurors herd. Every value has a job and a cost. The model looks healthier when each variable tracks the thing it is meant to track and refuses every other job.

The protocol's central loop is this: traffic pays for jurors, jurors settle contested traffic, public outcomes and consented tests grade jurors, and the grading makes the next verdict more expensive to buy. The game board above is just that loop with the attackers left on the table.
