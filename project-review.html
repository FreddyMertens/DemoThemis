Product definition

What this project is

This is a pre-product documentation and pitch website for two linked products, supported by interactive product models and a deliberately narrow DemoThemis MVP.

Demand engine

OmenMarketMaker

A prediction market anyone can open with a first stake. Markets begin as shared pools, can graduate into self-custodied YES and NO tokens, and may purchase disputed-outcome resolution from DemoThemis.

Neutral infrastructure

DemoThemis

A bullet proof escrow and arbitration layer for markets, apps, marketplaces, and online agreements. Verified humans are drawn after a dispute, vote privately, and produce the release rule for locked funds.

The pitch explains the intended product, not merely the demo. The demo MVP makes one core court journey tangible: one precise question, three verified humans, sealed answers, an on-chain majority, and fee distribution.

This review judges the public product story: whether the pages describe the same product, use the same rules, and carry a reader cleanly from promise to mechanism.

Overall verdict

The proposal describes one recognizable product system

The core thesis is coherent from the home page through governance: OmenMarketMaker can create early resolution demand and answer-key cases; DemoThemis independently prices each case from the work required and delivers neutral resolution.

The six chapters have distinct jobs and read in a sensible order. The run-through shows the journey, DemoThemis defines the court, Break the court tests its protections, OmenMarketMaker defines the market, the bootstrap chapter explains demand, and governance separates business control from truth.

The project does not need a conceptual rewrite. It needs two scope corrections and six small clarifications so every public surface describes the same design with the same numbers.

Confirmed findings

Where the public product story conflicts with itself

The first two findings can materially confuse a reader about the product. The remaining six are narrow mechanics or editorial fixes.

Finding 01High

The MVP is sometimes presented as the complete DemoThemis court

The MVP page calls its three-person commit/reveal sequence “The complete court mechanism” and says three humans reveal “the whole process.” The live About view also says “DemoThemis resolves objective yes-or-no questions.” The full DemoThemis chapter explicitly rejects commit/reveal and adds receipt-free ballots, 7/15/31-seat appeals, private reputation, rubric courts, and other protections.

Concise repair: say “the complete MVP court loop,” “the whole MVP flow,” and “this Demo MVP resolves objective yes-or-no questions.” This preserves the strength of the demonstration without redefining the full product around it.

Finding 02High

The run-through mixes the three-seat MVP with the full 7 → 15 → 31 ladder

The complete state map correctly says stakes choose 7, 15, or 31 seats. The guided product screens then draw three jurors, tally 2–1, and jump directly to a 31-juror appeal. That is neither the MVP path nor the full design.

Concise repair: make the guided full-product case use 7 seats and appeal to 15, or label the three-seat sequence as an MVP interlude and keep it out of the full-design appeal path.

Finding 03Medium

Two OmenMarketMaker sentences use rules the chapter has already replaced

The tokenization section rejects a simple volume threshold and defines a three-part Robustness Lock, but its comparison table still says the book is earned by “crossing the volume threshold.” The dispute section says tradeable claims permit “retroactive fixes,” while every finality section says settled money never reopens.

Concise repair: replace those phrases with “passing the Robustness Lock” and “longer challenge windows and fuller review before final settlement.”

Finding 04Medium

The early-bettor bonus needs one payout rule

The market is described as a pot whose odds are the raw ratio of the two sides and whose winners split it pro rata. The next sentence gives early claims a time-weight bonus, which changes either claim units, displayed odds, or payout shares. The reader is not told which.

Concise repair: state that early stakes mint extra weighted claim units and that both displayed odds and final pro-rata payouts use those weighted totals.

Finding 05Medium

“Curation bar” blurs the governance firewall

Governance gives the market token control of a “curation bar,” then says market-token holders cannot control aptitude tests. Everywhere else, curation means deciding which outcomes may grade jurors. Either the same term is being used for two different gates or business governance reaches into court quality.

Concise repair: if the business dial means listings or discovery, call it the “market listing bar” or “discovery bar.” Reserve “aptitude-test curation” for court governance.

Finding 06Medium

Difficult cases still lack a clear decline rule

Wrong-side votes can lose private reserve and a missed presence window creates a reliability ding, but no public page explains how a juror can decline a case outside their competence before accepting its risk. This matters most for rubric and community-standard courts.

Concise repair: add one rule: a drawn juror may decline during a short private acceptance window without a correctness penalty; deterministic standby replacement fills the seat, while repeated or late declines affect availability.

Finding 07Medium

The parlay funding explanation names no actual lender

Atomic fill-or-kill routing is understandable, but “flash-borrows the money from the order book” is not. The same paragraph says the book contains buyers for unwanted claims, not a lending facility. The value proposition is stronger than this implementation shorthand.

Concise repair: describe one atomic transaction that checks executable bids, combines the bettor’s stake with sale proceeds, mints the complete set, distributes the unwanted claims to those bids, and reverts unless every leg clears.

Finding 08Low

Small labels have drifted

OmenMarketMaker is called “six market features,” “a prediction market with 6 novel features,” and “open markets with unbuyable arbitration.” Its chapter still promises a 10-minute read despite containing roughly 3,600 visible words.

Concise repair: choose one stable product subtitle for repeated cards and change the reading estimate to 15 minutes.

Conclusion

Bottom line

The public project is coherent where it matters most. It presents a permissionless prediction market that creates demand for a verified-human arbitration product, and an arbitration product whose neutrality gives that market its strongest advantage.

The current problems are residue from combining a narrow demo MVP with an increasingly complete full design. Correcting that boundary, the stale panel numbers, and six short mechanics phrases will leave the site describing one product system without weakening its pitch.

No broad rewrite is justified. The strongest material is already present and the chapter structure works.
