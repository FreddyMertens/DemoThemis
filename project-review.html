Executive assessment

Where the project stands

The project is beyond the idea stage. DemoThemis has a public application, source-verified contracts, on-chain World ID verification, an operational question queue, and a browser sandbox for the larger mechanism.

Its two products are at different stages. DemoThemis is a deployed arbitration MVP plus a substantially broader protocol design. PredictionMoMo is a detailed product specification and demand strategy, but its market contracts are not built yet.

The strongest implemented achievement is the personhood-gated court path: a World ID proof is checked on-chain, a human-derived nullifier prevents a second seat, a panel is drawn after the question opens, jurors seal and reveal votes, and the contract resolves the result and routes funds.

Current verdict: a technically credible arbitration MVP with an unusually complete full-system design. The first live three-human case is the immediate proof point; scaled privacy, randomness, appeals, reputation, and PredictionMoMo are the next implementation layers.

Product state

Two products at different stages

Designed product

PredictionMoMo

The proposal defines open market creation from the first stake, self-custodied YES and NO claims, private fixed-odds rooms, trading during disputes, and peer-to-peer parlays.

Its strategic value is clear: it gives DemoThemis recurring cases, fees, and independently checkable outcomes. The current repository does not yet contain the market, order-book, tokenization, or governance contracts.

Deployed MVP + full design

DemoThemis

The MVP implements registration, bonding, panel selection, commit/reveal voting, fee routing, no-show penalties, and settlement through six verified contracts on World Chain mainnet.

The full design adds receipt-free ballots, private reputation and consequences, stronger randomness, expanding appeals, parallel panels, aptitude tests, and a paid watcher fast path.

Execution boundary

What runs now and what the full design adds

This is the most important current-state distinction. The MVP proves the basic on-chain court lifecycle. The scaled design supplies the protections behind the broader bullet proof and unbuyable claim.

Running on mainnet now

  • Production World ID verifier wiring and one-human-one-seat nullifiers
  • Wallet-bound registration, a 5 MUSD bond, and a three-seat panel
  • Two-step blockhash panel draw after the question opens
  • Public commit/reveal ballots and one automatic redraw
  • Question and deal escrow settlement with 70/20/10 routing
  • No-show slashing to the reward pool, never to winning jurors
  • World App interface, official question queue, and keeper

Designed and demonstrated in the sandbox

  • Receipt-free ballots and private payments, penalties, and reputation
  • VRF or drand randomness and per-draw presence checks
  • Panels of 7, 15, and 31 with funded appeals
  • Parallel non-overlapping panels for the largest cases
  • Aptitude scoring, private eligibility bands, and reward-pool payouts
  • Bonded assertions, paid watchers, and the optimistic fast path
  • PredictionMoMo markets, claim tokens, trading, parlays, and governance

Assessment

The project’s strongest assets

  1. 1

    A coherent two-product strategy

    PredictionMoMo is not an unrelated add-on. It supplies the court with paid demand and reality-anchored cases, while the court gives the market a differentiated resolution layer.

  2. 2

    Personhood is enforced in the contract path

    The World ID verifier, wallet binding, and spent nullifier are part of registration itself. The identity claim is therefore inspectable on-chain rather than delegated to a private backend.

  3. 3

    The mechanism is unusually well mapped

    The proposal connects identity, random selection, ballot privacy, incentives, appeals, scoring, governance, and demand. The run-through and attack model make those dependencies visible.

  4. 4

    The implementation is inspectable

    Contracts, tests, deployment records, application code, operational scripts, and the public repository give reviewers concrete evidence instead of a closed demo.

  5. 5

    The next milestone is operationally specific

    The portal, app, contracts, five-minute windows, queue, and keeper are prepared. The remaining capstone work is a phone smoke test, three human registrations, one official case, and permanent traces.

Recommended order

The shortest path from strong project to proven system

  1. 1

    Run the three-human mainnet capstone

    Complete the phone handoff, register three Production World ID humans, resolve official question one, preserve every explorer trace, and record the demonstration.

  2. 2

    Harden the court’s foundation

    Add production randomness, receipt-free ballots, deterministic replacement, and bounded finality before expanding the economic surface.

  3. 3

    Ship the scaled arbitration path

    Implement larger appeals, parallel panels, private reputation, aptitude tests, and reward-pool distribution as one integrated court lifecycle.

  4. 4

    Build the first PredictionMoMo market

    Use the resolver SDK to take one market from first stake through assertion, challenge, DemoThemis verdict, and payout.

  5. 5

    Validate the complete system independently

    Audit the contracts and privacy architecture, then test the juror and trader journeys with real users before increasing value at risk.

Conclusion

Bottom line

DemoThemis is currently a real, source-verified personhood-gated arbitration MVP backed by a much larger and coherent protocol design. PredictionMoMo is currently the designed product and demand engine that can turn the court into a recurring service.

The project’s next credibility leap is immediate and measurable: complete the first Production-human case. The leap after that is implementation: move receipt-free privacy, strong randomness, bounded finality, appeals, reputation, and the first market out of the sandbox and into the contract path.
