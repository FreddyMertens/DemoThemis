Governance

The firewall behind unbuyable arbitration

PredictionMoMo uses one-token-one-vote business governance. DemoThemis weights verified people and service behind rules that keep the court unbuyable.

The tokens 8 min read • Chapter 6 of 6

The split follows one question: is this governing a business or the truth? PredictionMoMo lets capital steer its own growth. DemoThemis counts verified people, tempers wealth with a square root, and rewards service. A constitutional firewall prevents either house from voting across the boundary or rerouting the court's invoice.

Governance adapts market margins, curation bars, graduation thresholds, juror compensation, and court windows. The diagram assigns every dial while keeping the load-bearing rules outside both ballots.

The company token

Capital governs growth, never truth

One-token-one-vote gives the business a direct mandate to compete. The market token steers growth spending, thresholds, its treasury, and a margin capped at one percentage point above the 2% court fee. The court's 70/20/10 invoice always routes first. Early traders receive a World-ID-gated genesis airdrop based on daily-net volume; afterward the token floats freely.

Larger investors receive more influence over the profit engine, aligning control with committed capital. The firewall confines that influence to the market business: investors can choose how aggressively it competes, while truth remains outside their reach.

Even total control of the market token reaches only its capped margin, treasury, and product settings. Verdicts, jurors, aptitude tests, court windows, and the court invoice remain beyond it. Business control stops at the courtroom door.

That containment matters because business governance has an ordinary correction: users, liquidity, and builders can leave a badly run market. A wrong court ruling cannot be repaired by switching products after escrow has settled.

The court token

One person's square root

Court weight is the square root of linked tokens, counted once per World ID. Voice therefore grows slower than wealth, while personhood closes square-root voting's usual loophole: a whale cannot take the root again through each new wallet. Linked balances sum before the root; unverified wallets can hold and trade tokens but have no vote.

Holding 100 times as many tokens gives 10 times the base weight. Every additional unit of voice becomes progressively more expensive before the service multiplier and per-person cap apply.

Personhood closes duplicate identities. The per-person cap contains collaborators, while receipt-free ballots let every recruited voter take the offer and privately disobey it.

With multipliers held at 1 and the 0.5% cap applied, splitting across unlinked wallets zeroes the whale's weight: unverified wallets do not vote, and linked wallets sum before the root.

A service multiplier then rewards fees paid and reputation-credited cases, not gross escrow or empty clicks. Both inputs decay on a 90-day half-life, their half-saturation points track live medians, and the multiplier tops out at ×4. No person may exceed 0.5% of court weight. Tokens must be linked for 30 days, with the snapshot fixed when a proposal opens.

Counting fees instead of escrow volume makes self-dealing costly: cycling funds through a private room burns the 2% fee each time. Credited cases use the court's baseline-adjusted score, so obvious or careless votes add little. Median-linked saturation prevents yesterday's activity scale from fixing tomorrow's electorate.

The exact reputation and case history remain private. A voter proves the allowed weight from committed inputs, so public governance does not recreate a link between identity, past cases, verdicts, and payments.

At 50,000 verified holders, a $0.10 token, and a 0.5% per-person cap, the controls show why capital alone cannot scale into control of the court.

The root and cap contain one rich person. Fake service burns fees into the system being attacked. The only strategy that scales is recruiting thousands of verified humans, each using the same receipt-free locked ballot as a verdict, so a buyer cannot verify delivery. The only scalable takeover path requires recruiting thousands of verified people whose receipt-free votes cannot be enforced. The attack becomes a vast, expensive coalition with no reliable way to buy delivery.

The rails

What a captured vote still could not do

Every adjustable parameter stays inside a hard-coded range, and passed proposals wait through a public 7-day timelock. No vote can reroute the invoice, cross the firewall, make court ballots provable, make court weight linear, remove personhood, reopen settled escrow, or redirect slashes away from the reward pool. The constitution is the part with no dial.

A narrow, public migration path can replace a faulty rule only through transparent notice and user choice. Neither house receives an emergency key capable of crossing the firewall or changing an active case.
