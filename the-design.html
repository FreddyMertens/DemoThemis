    Bootstrap loop

    Watch the market feed the court

    The run-through is one case, but the design only matters because the same loop repeats. Markets create work and fees. Fees pay jurors. Settled outcomes create answer keys. Better-scored jurors make the court safer. A safer court makes the next market easier to trust.

    Summary

    The whole system in one chain

    The market side

    A first bet creates a pot; fixed odds and private rooms make early liquidity usable; robust markets tokenize into self-custody claims; those claims keep trading through resolution and can be composed into peer-to-peer parlays.

    The court side

    A watched assertion settles most markets quietly. The contested few go through protocol escrow, random verified juries, private ballots, live reserves, verdict proofs, appeal floors, finality, and never-ending collusion reports.

    The loop

    Every settlement pays the people who make the next settlement possible. Every clean answer becomes training data. Every hard case adds public proof that the arbitration layer can survive pressure.

    The boundary

    The simulation is not a security proof. It is the product story: what happens, in what order, and which part of the system takes over when the happy path stops being happy.
