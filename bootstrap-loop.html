The bootstrap loop

How OmenMarketMaker can bootstrap an unbuyable court

As an early customer, OmenMarketMaker can purchase resolution work from DemoThemis; carefully checked outcomes then help the independent court prove its jurors and win outside disputes.

Bootstrap loop 4 min read • Chapter 5 of 6

1. The starting point

OmenMarketMaker starts the flywheel

When a market becomes eligible for resolution, any caller may post the deterministic court fee as a bond. OmenMarketMaker atomically sends DemoThemis a paid case while the outcome pool remains locked in the market. DemoThemis earns its service fee while OmenMarketMaker's market fee, margin, and treasury remain separate.

2. What markets provide

Integrated markets supply work and quality evidence

An eligible market supplies a real question while a caller's resolution bond pays DemoThemis automatically. A final YES or NO ruling reimburses that bond from YES and NO backing pro-rata; a final insufficient-information ruling leaves both sides' backing untouched. If the deterministic fee is at least the pool, no case opens and the market follows its precommitted cancellation rule. OmenMarketMaker's own market fee remains outside the court payment.

DemoThemis receipts fund its bench

At request time, the versioned fee policy calculates processing cost + required panel compensation + reward-pool top-up + capped operations charge. Panel pay follows workload and eligible supply, the reward-pool top-up falls to zero at target, and operations cannot exceed its approved public budget or per-case cap. The caller's bond pays the complete fee before jurors are selected.

Every completed case strengthens scoring

The scoring system rewards useful judgment beyond the obvious majority, consistency with the case's written criteria, and decisions that survive appeal. A later result—a final score, timestamped price, delivery record, or election—can add an independent check.

The same quality system applies to market outcomes, delivery specifications, and community-standard cases. The evidence available may differ, but every final case contributes.

3. Quality control

Every quality signal passes a reliability check

Case difficulty, written criteria, appeal outcomes, and later independent evidence are weighted by how much they reveal before updating a juror's record.

From completed case to quality evidence
01 Fix the standard

Lock the question, decision criteria, and evidence rules before anyone is drawn, so jurors are measured against the same task.

02 Compare every signal

Use difficulty-adjusted agreement, consistency with the criteria, appeal survival, and later independent evidence when available.

03 Update private histories

Every completed case adds evidence to the shared quality system. Exact scores and case histories stay private.

Every final case can update the jurors assigned to it. Later public outcomes add whole-court confirmation when reality provides one; cases without an external answer still contribute difficulty, criteria, and appeal evidence.

As volume grows, the court gets more evidence across more kinds of judgment without excluding any case from accuracy evaluation.

4. Expansion

A stronger court attracts the next customer

OmenMarketMaker can be an early customer. Fees paid for the cases it actually sends, plus measured results from those cases, can help DemoThemis build a performance record for other apps.

  1. 1

    Initial customers

    DemoThemis service fees pay jurors, every completed case updates one shared quality record, and public verdicts plus aggregate proofs build a verifiable record without exposing juror histories.

  2. 2

    Serve every kind of case

    Anyone can submit a well-defined case and fund its jurors. Escrowed work, creator payouts, marketplace refunds, governance questions, and stand-alone disputes are all possible uses.

  3. 3

    Become the default

    Signed verdict receipts, aggregate accuracy signals, and privacy-preserving proofs let new apps verify the court instead of rebuilding support desks.

Each stage adds both demand and evidence. The court can widen its customer base without changing the neutral rules that made its first verdicts useful.
