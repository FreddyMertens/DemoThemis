The bootstrap loop

How PredictionMoMo bootstraps an unbuyable court

PredictionMoMo creates paid resolution work from day one; carefully checked outcomes then help DemoThemis prove its jurors and win outside disputes.

Bootstrap loop 4 min read • Chapter 5 of 6

1. The starting point

PredictionMoMo starts the flywheel

PredictionMoMo gives DemoThemis paid cases, dependable revenue, and independently checkable outcomes from day one. Those outcomes identify strong jurors, stronger jurors win more apps, and every new customer brings the court more cases and revenue.

2. What markets provide

Each market supplies work, money, and answer keys

Every market gives DemoThemis a real question to settle and fee revenue. When the question has a clean, independently checkable result, its outcome can also become an aptitude test after curation.

The fees fund the bench

A 2% settlement fee routes 70% to juror pay, 20% to the reward pool, and 10% to operations. Revenue rises with use instead of committing the court to a fixed payroll.

Objective outcomes provide answer keys

For ordinary cases, the scoring system rewards useful judgment beyond the obvious majority guess. Objective results—a final score, timestamped price, or election—add an outside answer key that measures the whole panel against reality.

The aptitude stream selects precise, independently checkable outcomes. Rubric courts handle subjective questions, keeping every test matched to the kind of truth it can measure.

3. Quality control

Every aptitude test passes a quality gate

Watching, independent verification, and curation turn a clean settled outcome into a dependable answer key before it grades a juror.

From market result to aptitude test
01 Watch the window

A false assertion forfeits its bond to the first successful catcher, paying independent watchers to protect the answer stream.

02 Verify and curate

Check the source, wording, window coverage, and meaning, admitting only precise and independently verifiable outcomes.

03 Assign private test seats

Known-answer tests make up 5–20% of each juror's latest 50–150 cases. Exact grades and histories stay private.

One curated case can grade every juror assigned to it, so capacity is counted in test seats: cases × jurors. Public outcomes show whether the court as a whole stays accurate. Consented private-room cases can test individuals only when they did not participate and cannot look up the answer.

Watcher coverage and curation scale with volume, converting more settled cases into reliable test seats for a larger juror pool.

4. Expansion

A stronger court attracts the next customer

PredictionMoMo is the first rung. Its fees, cases, and measured results give DemoThemis the performance record that wins wider disputes from other apps.

  1. 1

    Markets only

    Market fees pay jurors, curated objective outcomes grade them, and public verdicts plus aggregate proofs build a verifiable record without exposing juror histories.

  2. 2

    Open the doors to real disputes

    The protocol adds escrowed work, creator payouts, marketplace refunds, and app-specific disputes.

  3. 3

    Become the default

    Signed verdict receipts, public-market accuracy, and privacy-preserving proofs let new apps verify the court instead of rebuilding support desks.

Each stage adds both demand and evidence. The court can widen its customer base without changing the neutral rules that made its first verdicts useful.
