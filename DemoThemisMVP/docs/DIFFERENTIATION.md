## The scan (2026-06-20)

**1. Grant RFPs / focus areas.** World's grant programs are **Spark** (early-stage
Mini Apps), **Scale** (growth), and **Developer Rewards** (usage-based). The page
names **no RFP topic or category** for dispute resolution, arbitration, escrow, or
trust infrastructure — use cases are open-ended under the broad themes of "digital
identity, global financial inclusion, and collaborative infrastructures."
→ *DemoThemis is a net-new capability, not an entry in a crowded, named category.*
Source: [world.org/grants](https://world.org/grants).

**2. Past grant recipients (Wave 0 ≈28 + Wave 1 ≈16, ~44 funded projects).** **None**
is a dispute-resolution system, arbitration court, juror mechanism, or escrow
protocol. The closest funded work is **one-human-one-vote governance / voting**:
- Wave 0 — *Clade Club* (merit-weighted DAO voting), *TheVoice* (majority/quadratic/
  conviction voting), *Votexx* (coercion-resistant voting), *Wonderland* (Sybil-proof
  voting, orb-verified addresses only), *Wallfacer / POPGDP* (sybil-resistant grant
  voting).
- Wave 1 — *zkSnap* (private voting), *Agora* (governance framework), *World Poll*
  (incentivized global polling).

These make **collective decisions**; none adjudicates a **two-party dispute** with
bonded jurors, evidence, and a binding settlement. Sources:
[Wave 1 recipients](https://world.org/blog/announcements/meet-worldcoin-wave1-community-grants-recipients) ·
[Wave 0 recipients](https://world.org/blog/world/worldcoin-builders-wave0-grant-recipients-advancing-tech-tree).

**3. World App Mini App catalog.** 150+ Mini Apps as of early 2025 (≈$8.8M WLD
transacted); the scan surfaced no dispute-resolution/arbitration app. P2P-trading and
marketplace flows exist (and use their own siloed, operator-run dispute desks) — these
are **potential customers** of a neutral court, not competitors.

**4. Decentralized arbitration as a category (off-World).** Live and respected, but
**capital-weighted**: Kleros selects jurors by **PNK stake** (sortition ∝ stake) and
UMA's DVM is **token-weighted** (1 UMA = 1 vote). Tellingly, even **Kleros's "Humanity
Court,"** which arbitrates *who is a unique human* (for Proof of Humanity), still
selects its **jurors by stake** — the registry is gated by personhood, the bench is
not. DemoThemis inverts this. Sources:
[Kleros Humanity Court](https://blog.kleros.io/complete-guide-to-staking-in-kleros-humanity-court/) ·
[Kleros whitepaper](https://kleros.io/assets/whitepaper.pdf).

**Honest scoping.** This reflects a single 2026-06-20 scan of the World App catalog
signal and both published grant waves — not an exhaustive crawl of all 150+ Mini Apps.
The category claim is therefore "none found in this scan," and the "first" claims are
scoped to "to our knowledge" + "in the World ecosystem," which the evidence supports.
We do **not** claim decentralized arbitration doesn't exist (Kleros and UMA do) — only
that no **personhood-gated** one does, and none in World.

## Differentiation paragraph (application-ready)

> Decentralized arbitration is a real, respected category — Kleros and UMA are live in
> production — but both decide by **capital**, so the same concentration of capital can
> influence many cases. DemoThemis removes stake-weighted jury power. Because the jury is gated by **personhood, not
> capital**, and the panel is drawn at random *after* the question from the whole
> verified-human pool, there is no known set of jurors to buy: to land a bribed majority
> on a specific case you would have to compromise a large share of the *entire* human
> pool and merely hope the draw seats enough of them. Larger pools reduce that probability;
> high-value cases add non-overlapping parallel panels and larger appeals. Receipt-free
> ballots prevent proof of a purchased vote. None of it is built on personhood — tellingly,
> even Kleros's "Humanity Court," which rules on *who is a unique human*, still selects its
> jurors **by stake**. Inside the World ecosystem the gap is starker: a June 2026 scan of
> the World App Mini App catalog and every World Community Grant recipient (Wave 0 and Wave
> 1) found **no dispute-resolution, arbitration, juror, or escrow project** — the closest
> funded work is one-human-one-vote *governance and voting* (Wonderland, Votexx, zkSnap,
> Agora, World Poll), which makes collective decisions, not two-party adjudications with
> bonds, evidence, and settlement. **DemoThemis inverts the decentralized-justice model —
> it uses proof-of-personhood, the primitive the World ecosystem is built on, as the juror
> gate itself**. Every case draws a fresh random panel, and high-value cases fan out to several
> non-overlapping panels that must all agree (capture odds multiply pᴺ), with any captured panel
> appealed into a larger, disjoint one. Once a juror serves on a dispute, no later panel or appeal
> in that dispute can draw them again. That makes it, to our knowledge, the first one-human-one-vote
> arbitration court and the first dispute-resolution app in World App, pairing on-chain World
> ID verification with bonded commit/reveal voting and atomic on-chain escrow settlement.

## One-line version (for the summary / pitch)

> The decentralized courts that exist (Kleros, UMA) pick jurors by **stake**, so a verdict
> has a price: buy a stake majority once and you decide every case after it for free. No
> World App or World-grant project does dispute resolution at all. DemoThemis gates the jury
> by **personhood instead of capital**, draws a fresh random panel after each question, keeps
> every panel and appeal within a dispute disjoint, and uses receipt-free ballots so purchased
> votes cannot be proven.
