0. Before You Start

Use this section to confirm the test environment is the local pseudo/testing MVP.

Local URL http://localhost:3000
Testing mode Pseudo/cohort mode: World Chain Sepolia cohort, MockSybilGate, read-only chain data.
Juror write testing Use /juror-preview. It is local-only and changes no chain state.
Important boundary: do not use the World App deep link for this local QA pass. Real World ID onboarding and real transactions are capstone-only. Local juror UX testing happens through the clearly labeled preview route.
If the server is not running: start the app from C:\dev\DemoThemisMVP\web in cohort mode, then open http://localhost:3000. If a tester only has this HTML open, ask the developer running the session to start the local server.
  • The app opens at http://localhost:3000.
  • The UI mentions the simulated Sepolia cohort or local QA where appropriate.
  • No tester is asked to install World App or verify World ID for this local pass.

1. Reviewer Landing Page

Goal: verify the first screen explains what DemoThemis is and gives a clear path into the demo.

/

  1. Open http://localhost:3000/.
  2. Read the headline and first paragraph. Confirm the product is understandable in under one minute.
  3. Click Watch attack demo to enter the sandbox.
  4. Return and click Open court to reach the cohort case list.
  5. Do not click Open in World App during local QA.
Expected: the landing page should frame the MVP as human arbitration for World App, show the evidence dashboard, and make the sandbox/court paths easy to find.

2. Sandbox Attack Demo

Goal: verify the pure-browser simulation is responsive and explains why token-weighted courts are easier to buy.

/sandbox

  1. Open http://localhost:3000/sandbox.
  2. Scroll to the comparative attack controls.
  3. Drag Attacker budget and confirm both columns update.
  4. Change Human pool width and watch the green curve shift.
  5. Click Draw a panel several times. The human court should sometimes redraw the random panel, not freeze.
  6. Continue down the page and test the core court, ladder, reputation, reward-pool, watchers, and case-browser widgets.
Expected: sliders and buttons respond immediately after the first route compile. Every widget is labeled simulated.

3. Cohort Court Home and Case Detail

Goal: verify testers can inspect pseudo cohort data and resolved case UX without wallet access.

/home

  1. Open http://localhost:3000/home.
  2. Confirm the banner says this is the simulated scale demo.
  3. Check the stats cards for simulated jurors, bonds held, and reward pool.
  4. Click Become a juror to test onboarding entry.
  5. Open a case card, for example /case/111.

/case/111

  1. Open a resolved case from the list.
  2. Confirm phase and case-type badges are legible.
  3. Confirm the case content and hash-check copy are visible.
  4. Check the verdict/result panel: it should explain unique humans, one vote each, and the 70/20/10 fee split.
  5. Scroll to the panel addresses and explorer links.
In cohort mode, commit/reveal buttons are disabled by design. Use the juror preview section below for full click-through UX testing.

4. Juror UX Without World ID

Goal: test the juror journey locally from onboarding through commit, reveal, missing salt, and resolved result.

/onboard

  1. Open http://localhost:3000/onboard.
  2. Review the three onboarding steps and sybil-rejection evidence panel.
  3. Scroll to the amber local-mode notice.
  4. Click Test juror UX locally.

/juror-preview

  1. Open http://localhost:3000/juror-preview.
  2. Confirm the yellow notice says local QA only and no chain state changes.
  3. Click Start local join preview.
  4. Confirm the page advances to Verified human #21 and then the commit screen.

Commit State

  1. Choose YES or NO.
  2. Confirm the commitment hash and saved salt preview are shown.
  3. Click Commit vote locally.

Reveal State

  1. Confirm the page remembers the committed vote.
  2. Click Reveal vote locally to finish the happy path.
  3. For an error-state check, click Preview missing-salt state instead.

Missing Salt State

  1. Click Preview missing-salt state.
  2. Confirm the reveal button disables or the page clearly explains why reveal cannot proceed.
  3. Click Reset preview to recover.
Expected: the tester should understand that the device-held salt is required to reveal.

Resolved State

  1. Use Reset preview, then Jump to commit if you want to retest quickly.
  2. Commit and reveal a vote.
  3. Confirm the resolved case shows a verdict, panel, one-person-one-vote copy, and fee split.

5. Dispute Opener

/dispute

7. What Testers Should Report

Use this checklist for feedback after completing the flows above.

  • Could you explain DemoThemis in one sentence after the landing page?
  • Did every local-mode warning feel honest rather than scary or broken?
  • Did the sandbox sliders and buttons respond quickly?
  • Could you find the case list and open a resolved case?
  • Did the juror preview make verify, bond, commit, reveal, and resolved states understandable?
  • Was the missing-salt state clear enough to know what went wrong?
  • Did any disabled button look like a bug rather than intentional local read-only behavior?
  • Were any labels, routes, or screenshots out of date?
Bug report template: route, device/browser, what you clicked, what you expected, what happened, screenshot if useful, and whether you were in local preview or read-only cohort mode.
