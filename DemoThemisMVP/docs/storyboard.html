What is live—and what the grant completes

Live MVP

The working core

  • Source-verified World Chain contracts
  • Production World ID gate: one human, one seat

Production-grade court

  • VRF/drand verified randomness
  • Receipt-free private ballots
  • Juror quality, case decline, and replacement

Before we show the MVP, here is the exact boundary. The deployed version proves on-chain personhood, a post-question panel draw, commit and reveal voting, majority resolution, fee distribution, escrow settlement, and a permanent receipt. The two challenges

Demand and decision quality

01
Where will demand come from before the court is large enough to be robust?
The bootstrap loop: early paid cases fund jurors and produce proof of decision quality; that evidence attracts more integrations, cases, and jurors.
02
How do we make arbitration consistently high quality?
Juror-quality mechanisms plus excellent arbitration UX: any verified human can join as a juror from day one, take any well-defined case they assess themselves as capable of arbitrating, build a quality record, and quickly start getting paid.
SHOT 12Problem and answer

The two challenges

Speaker: Founder 1

Two questions only: how the court bootstraps demand and how it produces consistently strong rulings.

Ask both questions first. Then reveal each answer in sequence rather than presenting them as assumptions.

The MVP proves the core flow, but a robust court must solve two further problems. First: where does demand come from before the network is large? Our bootstrap loop begins with paid cases, uses those fees to attract jurors, and turns completed rulings into evidence that attracts more customers. Second: how do we make arbitration high quality? We combine juror-quality mechanisms with excellent arbitration UX. Any verified human can join as a juror from day one, take any well-defined case they assess themselves as capable of arbitrating, build a quality record, and quickly start getting paid.
The Bootstrap loop chapter explains demand. The DemoThemis chapter explains juror scoring, case choice, and the arbitration experience.
