🤔What is this project?

DemoThemis is a demo of an online court: a way to settle disagreements by having a small group of verified real people vote — not bots, and not whoever has the most money. It was built to support a grant application (a request for funding).

The court is already built and running on a live network. The money in it is fake on purpose (nothing of value is at risk). Your job is to finish the setup and do one real test run with three real people, so reviewers can see it actually works.

Think of it like getting a new shop ready for opening day: the building is built — now you flip the switches, unlock the doors, and do a dry run with a few friends before the public arrives.

New word? Look here (glossary)

Plain-English meanings — open any time ▶
World ID
A way to prove you're a unique real person, using the World App on your phone. It doesn't reveal who you are — just that you're human and only counted once.
World App
The phone app where people verify with World ID and tap to approve actions. It quietly pays the tiny network fees for verified people.
The court / a "case"
A disagreement that people vote on. Two kinds here: a yes/no question, and an escrow deal dispute (two parties disagree about whether a payment should be released).
Juror
A verified person who has joined the court and can be randomly picked to vote on a case.
Commit & reveal
Voting in two steps so nobody can copy or buy your vote: first you submit a hidden vote (commit), later you unlock it (reveal). Each person does both.
Mainnet
The live, real network (as opposed to a practice network). Here it runs with fake money, but the network itself is the real one reviewers can inspect.
MockUSD
The pretend dollar used in the demo. Worthless on purpose — so no real money is ever at risk.
The deployer (wallet)
The project's own account that pays the tiny network fees for the setup actions you trigger. It holds a small amount of real network currency just for those fees.
Gas
The tiny fee to do anything on the network (a fraction of a cent here). World App covers it for the verified people; the deployer covers it for your setup steps.
Whitelisting
Telling World App "these are our official contracts — allow them." Without it, taps in the app are blocked.
Contract
A small program living on the network (the court, the token, etc.). There are six of them here.
Worldscan
A website where anyone can look up what happened on the live network (each action gets a link).
The Portal
Short for the World Developer Portal — World's website where the app is configured.
Terminal / command
A text window where you paste the grey command boxes and press Enter. See "What you'll need" above.
The sandbox
A play-area page on the website that simulates the whole idea with pretend players. Clearly labelled as a simulation.

Get your bearings

A 2-minute orientation before you change anything 0/3 ▶
In plain English: just confirm you understand three simple facts, so nothing later surprises you.
① Nothing here uses real money
The dollars in the demo are fake (called MockUSD). Nobody can lose anything of value. Relax — you can't break anything expensive.
② Two kinds of fees, and who pays them
Doing things on a live network costs a tiny fee ("gas"). The verified people pay nothing — World App covers them. You (using the project wallet) cover the fee for setup actions. It's already funded; the whole test run costs a few cents.
③ One rule about order
Two things must happen in order: Step 2 (whitelisting) before anyone taps in the app, and 3 people must join before a case can start. Everything else is flexible.
✓ You'll know you're ready when the three facts above make sense. That's it — no action needed yet.

⌖ Why
For safety, World App blocks any app from touching contracts it hasn't pre-approved. If you skip this, people tapping "join" will just see an error (invalid_contract). So we approve ours in advance.

  5. Click into the Permit2 Tokens box and paste the one MockUSD address.
  6. Click Save changes (the dark button, bottom-right). Wait a minute before testing.

Point the website at the live network

So the app people open is the real one 0/3 ▶
In plain English: flip one setting so the public website talks to the live court (not the practice one), then re-publish it. needs the owner's Vercel login
✓ Already done for you (19 Jun 2026). The new code is merged and deployed, and Production is set to the live network (NEXT_PUBLIC_CHAIN_ID=480). The site now opens to the funnel and the court reads mainnet. Just confirm the published site shows "A court of verified humans" (not a plain "Login with Wallet"), then tick the boxes.
⌖ Why
The website can show one of two networks. By default it shows a practice one. For real people to join the real court, the published site must point at the live network (its ID number is 480).
  1. Sign in to Vercel and open the project (demo-themis-mvp).
  2. Go to Settings → Environment Variables. For Production, set these two (add them if missing):

  3. Re-publish: open the Deployments tab and click Redeploy on the latest one.

Test it yourself first

Find every problem before you involve other people 0/4 ▶
In plain English: before you gather three busy people, prove the whole thing works by joining the court yourself, on your own phone. This catches problems while it's still easy to fix them.
✋ Most important advice on this page
Three people, each needed twice, is your scarcest resource. Don't let them be the ones who discover a missing setting. Test everything solo first.

4a · Join the court yourself (the best possible test)

If you have the World App with a verified World ID, be one of your three people and join first. This one action tests almost everything at once: the permissions (Step 2), the human check, and the fee sponsorship.

  1. you 📱Open the Mini App on your phone (World App must be installed). While the app is in preview, the reliable way in is the Portal's own test QR: go to Mini App → Permissions, and on the right you'll see a "See your mini app" card with a QR code — scan it with your phone's camera. (It's labelled "Developer Preview / for testing" — that's exactly what you want.)
  2. you 📱In the app, tap Become a juror → Verify with World ID & join. You'll see the World ID prompt, then "posting bond", then "You are a juror".
  3. you 💻Look at the "People joined as jurors" counter near the top of this page — it should tick up to 1.
✓ It worked if the counter shows 1 and the app says you're a juror.
▲ If joining fails — quick fixes
  • Error mentions invalid_contract → a permission is missing in Step 2 (most likely Permit2). Re-check and save again.
  • Nothing happens, or it looks like the wrong network → Step 3 isn't done (site not pointed at 480 yet).
  • A World ID error → just try the flow again; it sometimes needs a second attempt.

5

The real test run with 3 people

The main event — settle two cases for real 0/6 ▶

⚑ Needs: Steps 2–4 done, and 3 verified people together (a call or a room).

In plain English: three real people join the court, get randomly picked for a case, and vote to settle it — twice (once for a yes/no question, once for a deal dispute). You guide them and run a few commands.
✋ Tell each person this out loud
When you vote, your secret is saved in your own phone's browser. You must do the second step (revealing your vote) on the same phone, same app — don't switch devices, don't use a private/incognito window, don't clear your history in between. Otherwise your vote can't be counted.
⌖ Reading the steps below
you 💻 = you paste a command on the computer.   people 📱 = the three people tap in World App. Do one case completely before starting the next.

Case A — a yes/no question

  1. you 💻Give everyone plenty of time to vote: 
    wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh durations 600 600'
  2. people 📱Each person opens the app on their phone — the easiest way is to share the "See your mini app" QR from the Portal (Mini App → Permissions) for them to scan. Then each taps Become a juror → Verify & join. Watch the live "People joined" counter above reach 3.
  3. you 💻Open the question and pick the panel (it prints a case number — for the first case it's 0): 
    wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh open-question'
wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh draw 0'
  4. people 📱Each person opens the case and taps YES or NO → Commit your vote. (This hides their vote.)
  5. people 📱After a short wait, each person taps Reveal your vote (same phone!). Then you finish it: 
    wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh resolve 0'
✓ Case A is done when the app shows a result ("3 verified humans decided this") and the "Cases" counter above shows 1.

Case B — an escrow deal dispute

Same rhythm, but it starts as a payment deal that gets disputed. The case number this time is 1.

  1. you 💻Create the deal, dispute it, and pick the panel: 
    wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh open-escrow'
wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh draw 1'
  2. people 📱Same as before: each person commits, waits, then reveals (same phone).
  3. you 💻Finish it: 
    wsl -e bash -lc 'cd /mnt/c/dev/DemoThemisMVP && bash scripts/capstone-mainnet.sh resolve 1'
✓ Case B is done when the "Cases" counter shows 2 and both cases read "resolved".
★ How to run it smoothly
  • Get all three people on the same call. Say each phase out loud: "everyone commit now"… then a minute later "everyone reveal now".
  • Do Case A start-to-finish before touching Case B. Don't run both at once.
  • Keep a 4th person on standby in case someone drops off. A clean 3-out-of-3 makes the nicest demo.
  • If you can, screen-record this — you might capture footage you can reuse for the video (Step 6).

Last thing: save the proof links

After both cases, the project owner (or you) pastes the resulting web links into the file docs/DEMO.md, in the "Step 5 capstone" table. Those links are what the application points reviewers to. If you're unsure how, hand this off to the owner — it's a 5-minute copy-paste.

6

Record a short demo video

3–4 minutes — the thing reviewers actually watch 0/5 ▶
In plain English: a short screen-recording that tells the story: first the "you can't buy this court" idea, then a real person joining and voting on a phone. Most reviewers won't install anything, so this video does the convincing.

What to show, in order

  1. The hook (~20 sec). On the website's sandbox page, show the side-by-side demo: a money-based court gets "bought" and flips to the wrong answer; the human court doesn't budge. This is the most persuasive 20 seconds — lead with it.
  2. Joining for real (~80 sec). On a phone in World App: verify with World ID → join → get picked → vote (commit) → confirm (reveal) → see the result. After each on-screen action, briefly show its entry on the worldscan website so it's clearly real.
  3. Proof it's real (~50 sec). Show the contracts on worldscan, and the court refusing a fake or duplicate person (that's the "one person, one vote" promise in action).
  4. The history (~30 sec). The practice network's resolved cases on the explorer (say on screen that this part is a simulation).
  5. Close (~10 sec). Show the public web address and "open the sandbox, scan the QR, join the court."
★ Best way to record it (no fancy gear)
  • Computer screen: use a free recorder — OBS Studio, or QuickTime on a Mac, or the Loom browser app.
  • Phone screen: iPhone → plug into a Mac and use QuickTime "New Movie Recording" (pick the phone); or use the iPhone's built-in Screen Recording. Android → use the built-in screen recorder.
  • Record in short clips and stitch them together afterwards — much easier than one perfect take.
  • Use the 10-minute voting windows from Step 5 so you're never rushing on camera.
  • Captions are great (and you don't even need to speak). If you do narrate, keep it short and calm.
▲ Keep it honest (important for the grant)
  • Say or caption "test tokens — no real money".
  • When timers show, caption "demo mode: minutes instead of days" so the short windows aren't mistaken for the real design.
  • Label the practice-network part as a simulation.

Then: upload it (Loom gives an instant link, or YouTube as "unlisted") and give the link to the project owner to put in the docs and the application.
