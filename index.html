What we are building

A human court, bootstrapped by a prediction market.

PredictionMoMo brings questions, fees, and resolved cases for aptitude tests. DemoThemis brings escrow, verified jurors, and verdicts the biggest wallet cannot simply buy.

The products

PredictionMoMo markets, DemoThemis arbitration

Every prediction market needs a final ruling. Polymarket routes disputes through UMA's token-weighted vote; PredictionMoMo instead sends challenges to verified humans. That arbitration layer lets a market start tiny, stay private when needed, tokenize positions, and remain tradeable through a dispute. The market chapter explains the mechanics.

Product one

PredictionMoMo: six market features

  1. Challenged outcomes go to verified-human arbitration, not a token-weighted court.
  2. Liquid markets become self-custodied ERC-20 YES and NO tokens for liquidity, collateral, and leverage.
  3. Private invite-only markets offer fixed odds with third-party escrow and dispute resolution.
  4. An eligible, clearly worded market can open with less than a dollar.
  5. Claims remain tradeable through dispute windows, so holders can exit whenever liquidity exists.
  6. CTF token splitting enables peer-to-peer parlays, live odds, and early cashouts without bookie bankruptcy risk.
Product two

DemoThemis: protocol escrow with human arbitration

Apps provide the interface and demand; DemoThemis holds funds, resolves disputes, and releases or refunds them by rule. PredictionMoMo supplies its first cases and revenue.

The full design adds World-ID uniqueness, per-draw presence checks, sealed draws, receipt-free ballots, private reputation, sponsored gas, and expanding appeals. The deployed slice currently proves World ID registration wiring, commit/reveal voting, escrow settlement, and fee routing.

Together they separate the customer-facing market from neutral adjudication. PredictionMoMo is the court's first client, not its limit: marketplaces and online agreements can later use the same escrow and verdict layer without adopting the market.
