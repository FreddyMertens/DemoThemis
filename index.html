An arbitration layer for the open internet

What if no dispute could be bought?

Almost every online deal rests on someone trusted to settle the argument when it goes wrong. We are building that someone as a court of verified humans that money cannot capture, and paying for it with a prediction market.

Start here Seven chapters, four acts • About a 6 minute tour

Think about the last thing you bought from a stranger. A room for the night, a used phone, a ride across town.

You did not really trust the stranger. You trusted whoever sat behind them ready to take your side if the deal went bad. A platform that refunds you, a card issuer that claws the money back, a star rating that warns the next person. Pull that referee away and the whole trade stops feeling safe.

That referee is the most valuable and least examined part of the internet economy. It is also centralized, expensive, and easy to lean on if you are big enough. This series builds a different one from the ground up, then shows how to switch it on in the real world. The map below is the order to read it in.
